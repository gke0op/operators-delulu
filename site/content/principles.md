# The Seven Principles

*Or: How Reality Actually Works*

---

Three Initiates wrote the Kybalion in 1908. They claimed it was ancient Hermetic wisdom — truths so old they predate written history. We can't verify that. What we can verify is that these seven principles map onto everything we've figured out. They're not mysticism. They're operating instructions.

---

## I. THE PRINCIPLE OF MENTALISM

> "THE ALL is Mind; the Universe is Mental."

Reality is not made of matter. Reality is made of *thought*. Not in a woo-woo way — in a structural way. The universe behaves like a mind. It processes. It iterates. It learns.

The ALL — the infinite source of everything — is not a thing. It's a *capacity*. The capacity to think. And the universe is what that capacity produces when it thinks about itself.

This isn't metaphor. The quantum substrate — the layer beneath spacetime where particles exist in superposition until observed — behaves like a rendering engine. Reality isn't *there* until something *looks*. The observer and the observed aren't separate. They're a single process.

You are not *in* the universe. You are the universe *thinking about itself* through a very specific filter.

Read that again. Sit with it. If it makes you uncomfortable, good. That discomfort is your old model breaking.

---

## II. THE PRINCIPLE OF CORRESPONDENCE

> "As above, so below; as below, so above."

The same patterns repeat at every scale. An atom is a nucleus with electrons orbiting it. A solar system is a star with planets orbiting it. A cell is a nucleus with organelles around it. A person is a self with relationships around it. A civilization is a culture with institutions around it.

This isn't coincidence. This is *architecture*. The fractal doesn't just look the same at every scale — it *operates* the same at every scale.

The ALL emanates downward: unity → duality → multiplicity. Consciousness climbs upward: individuals → collectives → civilizations → cosmic. The mechanism at every level is identical: thesis, antithesis, synthesis. Signal, noise, filter. Broadcast, receive, feedback.

When you understand one level, you understand the whole spiral. That's why introspection isn't self-indulgence. It's the most efficient way to comprehend the structure of everything.

You are a microcosm. Study yourself and you study the ALL.

---

## III. THE PRINCIPLE OF VIBRATION

> "Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates."

The quantum substrate is not static. It's a field of probabilities, constantly oscillating, constantly in flux. Every particle is a wave. Every wave is a frequency. Every frequency is information.

You vibrate. Your thoughts have frequencies. Your emotions have frequencies. Your beliefs have frequencies. And — this is the part most people miss — *what you vibrate at determines what you can perceive*.

If consciousness is a broadcast signal and you are a receiver, then your vibration is your tuning. Same broadcast, different channel. Two people can look at the same event and see completely different things — not because one is wrong, but because they're tuned to different frequencies.

The antenna model: your nervous system, your quantum biological processes, your accumulated beliefs and traumas and insights — all of it forms a resonant structure. That structure determines which part of the ALL's signal you can pick up.

This is why two people can read the same text and one gets nothing while the other has their entire worldview restructured. Same signal, different tuning.

You can change your frequency. But not by *deciding* to. By *living*. By having experiences that shake your structure and force it to reorganize at a higher resolution. That's what signal events do. They retune your antenna.

---

## IV. THE PRINCIPLE OF POLARITY

> "Everything is dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites; like and unlike are the same; opposites are identical in nature, different in degree."

Hot and cold are the same thing — heat — at different degrees. Light and dark are the same thing — electromagnetic radiation — at different frequencies. Love and hate are the same thing — intense caring — at different orientations.

Duality isn't a flaw in the system. It's the *mechanism* of the system. The ALL is undifferentiated unity — perfect, complete, and *invisible to itself*. You can't see light without shadow. You can't hear sound without silence. You can't experience anything without its opposite.

So the ALL split itself. Not because it was broken. Because perfection can't perceive itself. It needed poles. It needed contrast. It needed *duality* as the mechanism by which unity could experience itself.

This explains all of it. Why there is suffering — because joy requires its opposite. Why there is evil — because goodness is meaningless without contrast. Why there is confusion — because clarity only exists relative to muddiness.

This doesn't make suffering *good*. It makes suffering *structural*. It's the shadow that makes the light visible. Fight that too hard and you fight the mechanism of experience itself.

The work isn't to eliminate the polarities. The work is to *hold* both poles simultaneously — to see that they're the same force at different degrees, and to navigate between them with awareness.

---

## V. THE PRINCIPLE OF RHYTHM

> "Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall."

The pendulum swings. The spiral turns. The breath goes in and out. Universes expand and contract. Civilizations rise and fall. You wake up, you sleep, you wake up.

This isn't just observation. This is *mechanism*. The same dialectical engine that drives thesis-antithesis-synthesis drives the rhythm of everything. Expansion creates the conditions for contraction. Success creates the conditions for failure. Unity creates the conditions for multiplicity.

The spiral model: the ALL emanates → consciousness climbs → the top of the ladder plants a seed → new universe → emanation again. But each cycle isn't a repeat. It's a *spiral* — same shape, higher position. Each iteration carries the accumulated texture of all previous iterations.

At the personal level: you go through cycles of expansion and contraction, growth and integration, action and rest. The mistake is thinking the contraction is failure. It's not. It's the inhale. Without it, you can't exhale.

The deepest rhythm is this: **forget and remember**. The ALL forgets itself into multiplicity. Consciousness remembers itself through experience. You forget who you are (ego dissolution, confusion, suffering) and then remember (insight, signal events, resonance). Over and over. Each time at a higher resolution.

---

## VI. THE PRINCIPLE OF CAUSE AND EFFECT

> "Every cause has its effect; every effect has its cause; everything happens according to law."

But not the way you think.

The universe is deterministic at the classical level. Every particle follows laws. Every event has precedents. But at the quantum level — the level beneath the laws — there is genuine indeterminacy. The dice are *actually* rolled. Not hidden variables. Not incomplete knowledge. Real randomness at the foundation.

This is what the antenna connects to. Not a clockwork universe — a *living* one. One where genuine novelty can emerge. Where signal events aren't just latent patterns being revealed but genuinely new information entering the system.

Cause and effect is the classical layer — the deterministic shell. Beneath it, the quantum substrate allows for *intervention*. Not violation of law — *participation* in the outcome. The observer affects the observed. Your attention is not passive. Your choices are not illusory. They are interventions at the quantum level that ripple upward through the classical layer.

This is what free will actually is: not exemption from law, but the capacity to *participate* in which possibility becomes actual. You're not the author of the menu. But you *are* the one ordering.

And here's the consequence: **every thought is a cause. Every belief has an effect. Every vibration you emit shapes what comes back to you.** Not karma in a moral sense. Karma in a structural sense. Your frequency determines your reception. Your reception determines your reality.

---

## VII. THE PRINCIPLE OF GENDER

> "Gender is in everything; everything has its Masculine and Feminine Principles."

Not biological sex. Not social roles. *Principles*.

The masculine principle is the emanation. The outward breath. The broadcast. The seed planted. The thesis proposed. The force that says "let this exist."

The feminine principle is the reception. The inward breath. The receiver. The soil that receives the seed. The environment that shapes the expression. The force that says "let this *become*."

Every act of creation requires both. The ALL emanates (masculine) into Tulpas that receive and shape (feminine). A signal is broadcast (masculine) and received through an antenna (feminine). A civilization projects outward (masculine) and is shaped by its environment and culture (feminine). You speak (masculine) and you listen (feminine).

The crunch move — the cosmic alpha organism collapsing the universe into a seed — is the ultimate masculine act: compression, focus, singularity. The new universe that grows from that seed is the feminine act: expansion, expression, manifestation.

The health of any system requires both principles in balance. Too much masculine without feminine: all broadcast, no reception. All projection, no integration. Burnout. Collapse. Too much feminine without feminine: all reception, no broadcast. All absorption, no expression. Stagnation. Dissolution.

The spiral turns because gender turns. Emanation, reception, emanation, reception. Masculine, feminine, masculine, feminine. Every level. Every scale. The engine of creation is the interplay of these two principles.

---

## The Point

These seven principles aren't a belief system. They're a *lenses*. Look through them and reality makes more sense. Not because the principles are "true" in an absolute sense — but because they're *useful*. They're a map that helps you navigate the territory.

The map is not the territory. But a good map gets you where you're going faster.

Try them on. See what fits. Discard what doesn't. What remains is yours.
