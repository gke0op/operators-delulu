The Lab

Tools for the journey

Everything so far has been theory. Maps. Frameworks. Cosmology.

This page is different. This is where you use the tools.

The Lab is the daily practice. The vagus training, the signal journal, the resonance test, the parasite purge — these are the things you do every day to keep your antenna tuned and your container stable. The Gateway is the deep practice. When your daily practice has prepared you, the Gateway takes you into the psychic realm directly. The Lab is the foundation. The Gateway is the cathedral. Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient alone.

The Resonance Test

What frequency are you tuned to?

There are no right answers. This isn't a personality quiz. It's a mirror. Answer honestly and it'll show you something about your current tuning.

The Egregore Detector

Is your life serving you, or are you serving something else?

For each area of your life, ask these questions:

Your Job

  • Does your work produce something you believe in?
  • If your boss/company/product disappeared tomorrow, would the world be worse off?
  • Do you feel more like yourself at work, or less?
  • Would you do this work for free? (Not "could you afford to" — would you choose to?)
  • When you talk about your work, do you feel pride or just... habit?
If most answers are "no" or "I don't know": You might be feeding a dead egregore. Your labor is fueling something that doesn't serve you. That doesn't mean quit tomorrow. But it means notice.

Your Consumption

  • What did you consume today? (Media, food, content, products)
  • Why did you consume it?
  • Did you choose it, or did the algorithm / habit / social pressure choose it?
  • After consuming, do you feel more alive or less?
  • Could you go a week without it?
If most consumption is habitual without satisfaction: You're being farmed. Your attention, your time, your money — feeding organisms that use you as fuel. Again: notice first. Change comes from awareness.

Your Relationships

  • Who in your life challenges you?
  • Who in your life comforts you?
  • Are those the same people?
  • When was the last time someone told you something you didn't want to hear and you listened?
  • Do your relationships expand you or maintain you?
If everyone in your life agrees with you: You're in an echo chamber. Not a conspiracy — just a tuning problem. Seek dissonance deliberately. It's the only way to grow.

Your Beliefs

  • What do you believe that you've never questioned?
  • What do you believe because you concluded it, and what do you believe because you absorbed it?
  • When was the last time you changed your mind about something that matters?
  • What would you believe if you'd been born in a different place, different time, different culture?
If you can't identify any beliefs you've absorbed uncritically: You're not thinking. You're echoing. Everyone absorbs beliefs uncritically — that's how culture works. The question is whether you ever examine them.

The Signal Journal

Track your kurtosis events.

Every day, ask yourself:

  1. What surprised me today? Not what I expected. What actually happened that I didn't predict.
  2. When did I feel most alive today? Not most comfortable. Most alive.
  3. What did I learn today that I didn't know yesterday? Not information — understanding. What clicked?
  4. When was I on autopilot today? What was I doing? How did it feel?
  5. What am I avoiding? What conversation, what decision, what truth am I putting off?

You don't need to write essays. A sentence per question is enough. The point is to build the habit of noticing. Most people go through entire weeks without a single conscious signal event. Not because signal events aren't happening — because they're not tracked.

After a month of this, look back. Patterns will emerge. You'll see your frequency. You'll see where you're stuck. You'll see where the signal is.

After three months, you'll have a map of your own psyche that's more detailed than most people's after a lifetime.

After a year, you won't need the journal anymore. The noticing will be automatic. You'll be tuned.

The Parasite Purge

A practice, not a one-time exercise.

Once a week, choose one thing you're going to stop feeding for seven days:

  • One app
  • One subscription
  • One habit
  • One relationship interaction pattern
  • One belief you've been carrying without examining

Don't announce it. Don't make it a performance. Just... stop feeding it for seven days.

Observe what happens. Notice the craving. Notice the anxiety. Notice what rushes in to fill the gap. Notice what you discover when the noise stops.

Most parasites can't survive seven days without feeding. If the thing you stopped feeding feels angry about being starved — if it sends you notifications, if it creates anxiety, if it actively pulls you back — you just identified something that was using you.

What you do with that information is up to you.

The Mirror

The hardest tool on this page.

Find a quiet moment. Sit with this question:

If everything I believe about reality is wrong — what's the first thing I'd need to let go of?

Don't answer immediately. Sit with it. Let the question work on you. The answer might come now. It might come in three days. It might come as a dream.

But when it comes, you'll know. And letting go of it will be the most important thing you've ever done.
